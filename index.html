Vittore Branca (1913─2004)

This page celebrates the life and works of renowned Italian philologist Vittore Branca.

A chronology of Branca's life.

  • 1913: Vittore Branca is born in Savona, Italy and is raised nearby Como Lake.
  • 1931: He is admitted at the prestigious Scuola Normale Superiore in Pisa. He meets Giovanni Gentile, his future supervisor and mentor.
  • 1935: Branca graduates from the Scuola Normale Superiore with the highest mark.
  • 1937: Branca works at the Accademia della Crusca in Florence to the national edition of Giovanni Boccaccio's works. He starts to teach in local high schools.
  • 1943: Branca works with other Catholic intellectuals to the Codice di Camalodoli, a document that would shape the establishment of the Christian Democratic political party after the war. Branca also collaborates with the Italian Resistance against Nazism and Fascism.
  • 1944: Giovanni Gentile contacts Branca to collaborate with him, but Branca refuses. Branca takes part in the events that will bring to the liberation of Florence, being the only survivor of the Resistance's leadership.
  • 1944-1949: He teaches at the Universities of Florence and Rome.
  • 1952-1953: He is Visiting Professor at the Sorbonne University in Paris
  • 1953: He starts his career at the University of Padua, where he will remain for the rest of his life
  • 2004: Branca dies in Venice at 91 years old.
